Your Survival Manual

So You're Going to
a Café.

Congratulations. Here's everything that will happen to you, whether you like it or not.

Going to a café sounds simple. You walk in, you order coffee, you leave happy. And sometimes — on a magical Tuesday when the planets align and the barista had a good night's sleep — that is exactly what happens. The rest of the time, this guide applies.

🚪

01 — The Entrance

You Will Stand in the Doorway Too Long

You'll push when you should pull. You'll pull when you should push. The door will have no handle, only a flat plate, and you will still try to grab it. Someone inside will watch you do this. They will say nothing. You will make eye contact. This is the café experience beginning.

😶 Estimated duration: 8–45 seconds
📋

02 — The Menu

You Will Panic-Order Something You Didn't Want

You had a plan. A flat white, maybe a pastry. But then the board had seventeen options written in chalk at slightly different angles and the person behind you was breathing loudly and you said "oat latte" when you meant "Americano" and now here you are, holding an oat latte, wondering who you are as a person.

☕ Happens to literally everyone
💻

03 — The Seating

The Table You Want Is Always Taken

The window seat. The cosy corner booth. The one with the good natural light. All occupied — by a single person with a laptop, three empty cups, and no apparent intention of leaving before sunset. You will take the wobbly table near the bathroom. You will pretend this is fine.

🪑 Window seat success rate: 11%
📶

04 — The WiFi

The Password Will Be Unhinged

It's on a chalkboard somewhere. You'll look. You won't find it. You'll ask. The barista will recite it with the energy of someone who has said it five hundred times: "CoffeeL0ver$2019exclamation". You will type it wrong three times. Your phone will say "Incorrect Password." The barista has already walked away.

📡 Average connection attempts: 4
🎵

05 — The Music

It Will Be Exactly the Right Volume to Be Annoying

Not loud enough to enjoy. Not quiet enough to ignore. Just present enough that you hear every fourth word of whatever song is playing and spend ten minutes trying to remember if you like it. Bonus: if you sit for long enough, the same playlist will loop, and you will hear the same song you couldn't identify the first time.

🎧 Genre: "Indie Chill Café Vol. 47"
🥐

06 — The Pastry

You Will Order It. You Will Not Regret It.

You told yourself just coffee. Then you saw the almond croissant under the glass dome, all golden and flaky, dusted with icing sugar, catching the light like a small miracle. You didn't stand a chance. Here's the thing though: this is the right decision. The pastry is always the right decision. Order the pastry.

✅ The one thing that always goes well
🍵

07 — The Name Situation

They Will Mishear Your Name

You said "James." They wrote "Janice." You said "Sarah." The cup says "Sera." Nobody knows how "Dave" became "Bave" but here we are. You will briefly consider correcting them, decide it's not worth it, and spend the next ten minutes hoping nobody else named Bave shows up.

🖊️ Name accuracy rate: roughly 60%
⏱️

08 — The Time

You Will Stay Much Longer Than Planned

You said fifteen minutes. Maybe twenty. The coffee arrived warm, the seat was comfortable, the background hum was oddly soothing, and at some point your brain just… unclenched. That was ninety minutes ago. This is not a problem. This is the entire point of a café. You've done something right for once.

🕰️ Planned: 20 min. Actual: 1hr 45min.

The Unofficial Café Survival Guide

Condensed wisdom from years of fieldwork

  • → Always look at the door before you touch it. There are clues.
  • → If you don't know what to order, say "what do you recommend?" and just go with it. You can do this.
  • → The WiFi password is case sensitive. It is always case sensitive.
  • → Bring headphones. Not necessarily to listen to anything — just as emotional armour.
  • → Tip if you can. The person who made your coffee has heard "CoffeeL0ver$2019exclamation" two hundred times today.
  • → If the pastry looks good, get the pastry. We cannot stress this enough.
  • → Accept that you will stay longer than you meant to. Pack accordingly.

Which Café Person Are You?

Answer honestly. We will not judge. (We will judge a little.)

When you sit down at a café, the first thing you do is…

A Open your laptop immediately and place it at an angle that implies you're very busy
B Look around slowly, taking in the decor, and nod approvingly to no one
C Read the entire menu twice before deciding you want a flat white, which you always get
D Immediately locate where the pastries are and begin planning your approach

← Pick one to reveal your café identity

At the end of the day, a café is just four walls, some good beans, and a room full of people pretending to be more productive than they are. It's one of the best things humans have ever invented. Go enjoy one.
